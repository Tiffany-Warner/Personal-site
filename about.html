A Little About My Background

I was always fascinated by how things work which is what drew me to biology. The human body, especially the nervous system, and everything else in the world has a structure and is part of a complex system. It’s the job of scientists to figure out exactly how it all works and then manipulate or utilize those mechanisms to do something great for the world. After graduating from Grand Canyon University with a B.S. in Biology, I went to work for a medical device manufacturing company. Here, I got to be part of a process that was involved in saving people’s lives. It was here that I was first exposed to engineers and engineering. It became very clear to me that the engineers were the people who spent most of their time trying to figure out how to create and manipulate processes, machines, and software to make our process easier, more efficient, and more error-proof.

Why Computer Science?

Software played a huge part in this. Anytime a new software technology was implemented, the efficiency of the process was drastically improved. For some unknown reason, I had always assumed coding would be outside the scope of my abilities. It wasn’t until I decided to go back to college to pursue an engineering degree that I first got introduced to coding with a MATLAB course from Northern Arizona University. It was by far my favorite course because I felt like between me and my computer, I had the power to create something from anywhere at anytime. A simple dice game was my favorite project. I was so proud of this tiny little program. For years, I’ve been drawing as a hobby. As an artist, I always felt so proud knowing I brought something into existence. I created something.

I got this same feeling when I created that dice game.

I had to learn more. So, I started searching for the best options to get a C.S. degree and that’s when I found Oregon State University’s Ecampus C.S degree. OSU has been ranked in the top 10 for Online Programs several years in a row. All exams were proctored and it had a great reputation. I knew I could get a solid education and the quarter system provided the flexibility I needed.

Where I plan to go from here

I'm very excited to be getting the foundation I need to build Web applications. I'm also very interested in Mobile App Development. I haven't fully decided on a path since I'm still exploring the different routes and technologies.
